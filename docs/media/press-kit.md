## Boilerplate Copy

### Short (50 words)

Reach is an open-source deterministic decision engine that brings cryptographic provenance and bit-identical replay to AI systems. It ensures that decisions are auditable, verifiable, and reproducible‚Äîessential requirements for production AI in regulated industries. Learn more at reach.dev.

### Medium (100 words)

Reach is a high-performance, deterministic decision engine for autonomous agents and complex workflows. Unlike traditional AI systems that operate as black boxes, Reach provides cryptographic provenance and bit-identical replayability for every decision.

The engine evaluates scenarios under uncertainty, applies configurable policies, and produces decisions that can be independently verified and exactly replayed. Same input always produces the same output, across runs and platforms.

Built in Rust and Go, Reach is MIT licensed and designed for mission-critical applications in finance, healthcare, and infrastructure where decision accountability is paramount. Visit github.com/reach/reach to get started.

### Long (200 words)

Reach is a production-grade deterministic decision engine that solves the accountability problem in AI-powered systems. While traditional AI provides powerful capabilities, it often operates as an opaque black box‚Äîmaking decisions that cannot be reproduced, audited, or verified. Reach changes this paradigm by ensuring every decision is cryptographically proven and bit-identically replayable.

At its core, Reach evaluates decision scenarios using expected utility theory, applying user-defined policies to ensure safety constraints are never violated. The engine produces a cryptographic fingerprint for every decision‚Äîa SHA-256 hash that uniquely identifies the exact input, execution path, and output. This fingerprint enables unprecedented transparency: any decision can be replayed exactly as it happened, on any platform, at any time.

Reach is designed for mission-critical applications where decision accountability matters. Financial institutions use it to prove trading algorithm decisions for compliance. Healthcare providers audit AI diagnostic recommendations. Infrastructure teams automate scaling with full accountability. Autonomous system developers replay incident decisions for root cause analysis.

The engine is built for performance and portability. Core components are written in Rust for speed, with Go services for orchestration. It runs on Linux, macOS, and Windows with identical results. The MIT license encourages adoption and contribution.

Reach represents a fundamental shift: from trusting AI to proving AI. In a world increasingly powered by algorithmic decisions, this transparency isn't just valuable‚Äîit's essential.
